MUSICAL MODE

In the theory of Western music, a mode is a type of musical scale coupled with a set of characteristic melodic behaviors.

Origin


Musical modes have been a part of western musical thought since the Middle Ages, and were inspired by the theory of ancient Greek music. The name mode derives from the Latin word modus, "measure, standard, manner, way, size, limit of quantity, method".

QUICK INTRO


I am Pretty sure You are familiar with the music scale we hear about in our everyday life. Without any other specification, we usually associate them with the following notes:

 Do Re Mi Fa Sol La Ti 
 C  D  E  F  G   A  B  

If we order these notes from the lowest(C) to the highest(B), then we will have the natural Major scale. When we vary the Music Modes, we are simply change the starting lowest note in such pattern. Because there are 7 different notes, naturally we have 7 types of Music Modes. Based on which note is the lowest, they can be represented as:

I.   > Ionian 
II.  > Dorian 
III. > Phrygian 
IV.  > Lydian 
V.   > Mixolydian 
VI.  > Aeolian 
VII. > Locrian 

INTERVAL/STEP


Pitch measures how "high" or "low" a sound is.

We can first talk about a relatively familiar concept: Melody.
Melody is basically a sequence of music notes, the way it sounds a certain way is actually more dependent on the relative pitch difference between the notes, but not the pitch of each note. We can denote the difference in pitch as Interval, which can be measured in the unit Step. There are essentially two types of Steps:

 Whole Step = 2 x Half Step 

Every pair of 2 consecutive notes does not have the same interval. For example, The interval of C-D is a whole stap, while the interval of E-F is a half step. We can represent a half step as -.

For Ionian, which is the natural key,the intervals among the 7 notes:

 C--D--E-F--G--A--B-C'  Or  2 2 1 2 2 2 1 

For Dorian, it will become:

 D--E-F--G--A--B-C'--D'  Or  2 1 2 2 2 1 2 

Therefore, when we change the music mode, we basically change the structure of a melody, and naturally it will sound different.
